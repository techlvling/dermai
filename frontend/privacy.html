Privacy & Data

Last updated: April 2026

Short version: Your photo is sent to Google Gemini for analysis, then immediately deleted from our server. We store nothing about you. Your scan history lives only on your device.

What happens to your photo

  • When you upload a selfie, it is temporarily saved to our server's memory to be forwarded to Google Gemini's Vision API.
  • Once Gemini returns the analysis result, the image file is permanently deleted from our server. This happens in the same request — your photo is never stored beyond a few seconds.
  • We do not log, save, or back up uploaded images at any point.

Google Gemini

  • Your image is processed by Google's Gemini Vision model via their API. Google's own data handling policies apply to this step.
  • Per Google's API terms, images sent via the API are not used to train their models by default.
  • You can review Google's API data usage policy at ai.google.dev/gemini-api/terms.

What we store on your device

  • Latest analysis (dermAI_analysis): your most recent skin score, type, and concerns. Used to populate the recommendations page. Cleared when you scan again.
  • Scan history (dermAI_history): the last 20 analysis results with dates and health scores, stored as JSON in your browser's localStorage. Used only to show your progress chart. Never transmitted anywhere.

You can clear this data at any time by opening your browser's developer tools and running localStorage.clear(), or by clearing your browser's site data for this domain.

Product recommendations

  • Recommendations are generated client-side by matching your skin concerns to our local product database. No personal data is sent to any third party for this step.
  • The "Search on Amazon" links are plain search queries — they are not affiliate links and do not include tracking parameters.

Analytics & tracking

We do not use any analytics, tracking scripts, cookies, or advertising on this site.

Medical disclaimer

DermAI provides cosmetic skin analysis only and is not a medical diagnosis tool. The analysis is generated by an AI vision model and may not accurately reflect your skin condition. Always consult a board-certified dermatologist for skin health concerns.
